Embedding r-Factorizations of Complete Uniform Hypergraphs into s-Factorizations

My master's thesis deals with the existence and nonexistence of a certain mathematical structure. This problem was presented to me as a generalization of a problem posed by Australian mathematician Peter J. Cameron. I had the opportunity to make significant advancements on this problem, introducing a wide variety of new ideas to this problem with which I have admittedly become obsessed!

In the process of working on this problem, I discovered that much can be determined about the existence of the aforementioned mathematical structure by investigating a simpler mathematical structure, namely what I've elected to call k-irreducible sums. Simply put, for a positive integer k, a k-irreducible sum is a sum of proper divisors of k (that is, positive divisors of k other than k itself) whose total is a multiple of k such that no sub-sum has a total of exactly k. For instance, the sum 1 + 6 + 6 + 6 + 6 + 10+ 10 + 15 is a sum of proper divisors of 30 with a total of 60, and it's impossible to pick and choose terms of this sum in order to obtain a total of exactly 30. This makes it a 30-irreducible sum. Investigating the existence (and more importantly the non-existence) of k-irreducible sums for various classes of integers has proven extremely useful in constructing the mathematical structure in which we're interested.

My thesis contains Python scripts that run searches and perform computations relevant to k-irreducible sums. The Python scripts from my thesis can be found in this GitHub repository.. See chapter 3 of my thesis to learn the fundamentals of k-irreducible sums. While the thesis as a whole requires a solid foundation in combinatorics and mathematics as a whole, I made a point of writing the chapter on k-irreducible sums in such a way that it can be understood without prior knowledge of combinatorics.
